Our Mission

Global Village International (GVI) is dedicated to helping women and children in the developing world by providing a hand up to spur their faith and livelihood through health, education and employment.

Who We Are

Husband and wife Romalene Tolentino and Daniel Thompson founded GVI in December 2009 in Henderson, Nevada. Inspired by her grandmother and pastor, Romalene sought a way to leave the world in a better place. And after 20+ years of successful experience in the finance industry, the couple also wanted to focus their charitable giving. Romalene and Dan set the foundation’s overall tone and direction.

How We Work

Grant Guidelines

Global Village International provides financial support through its grant application process to organizations providing service in the areas of focus outlined in our mission statement: health, education and/or employment of women and children in the developing world.

Further Guidelines

  • Wherever possible, contributions should be programmatic in nature and focus, and add value to the scope of services offered by an organization. The benefits of the program should be detailed, quantifiable and included as part of the grant proposal.
  • Organizations may apply for full or partial funding of a particular program.
  • Only one grant will be awarded to a non-profit organization per calendar year. If renewed funding is desired, the organization must submit a new proposal.
  • Grants are typically not made for general operating efforts or to create endowments.
  • Multi-year commitments are strongly discouraged.

Eligibility Requirements

The Global Village International charitable foundation funds only charitable, non-profit organizations as defined by section 501(c)(3) of the IRS tax code, public schools and other qualified state or local governmental entities.

Currently, we do not accept unsolicited proposals.

If you feel that your organization and GVI’s missions are aligned, feel free to email us your website address or contact information, so we can become better acquainted with the great work you are doing.
